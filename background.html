Background

  • Rumors, Fake News, Misinformation, and Disinformation (refer as fake news in the below) are long existing issues that can be traced back to shortly after the printing press was invented in 1439
  • In this digital age, information can transmit in a very fast way, so does fake news
  • Hong Kong also facing the threat of these fake news, especially the social conflict aspect

Fake News and Hong Kong

  • Fake news has been in Hong Kong for years
  • People forward the message easily because they believe it would be better to stay alert to the disinformation
  • Since the social unrest in 2019, fake news becomes a more serious issue
  • Some people claim that even news media report fake news

Fake News and Health

Screenshot of News Lancet, Cable News
  • Health information is a very common kind of fake news
  • Often without any evidence to back it up, or the expert vouch it is fake
  • Sometime it is backup by celebrity which didn't really vouch for it
  • During SARS pandemic, a 14-year-old created a fake news site, claiming that Hong Kong had been declared infected area by the Chief Executive
  • After the outbreak of the COVID-19, fake news about health aspect have boasted

Fake News and Election

  • In 2016 US presidental election, millions of people exposured to fake news, such as stories proclaiming that Pope Francis has endorsed Donald Trump for U.S. president.
  • During Taiwan's 2020 election, videos of disinformation were sent during the same period in different Line groups
